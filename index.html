VTOKU Cam turns your iPhone or iPad into a virtual-production camera — track the real world with ARKit, drive and film a VRM avatar in AR, and stream camera motion and video straight into Warudo.

Get started →

One camera, the whole pipeline

Everything a VTuber or virtual-production set needs to put a phone on the rig and go live.

ARKit camera tracking

World-tracked 6-DoF pose at 60 Hz, phase-locked to the camera, with real lens FOV derived from the device intrinsics.

VMC & FreeD streaming

Send camera pose to Warudo over VMC/OSC and to any FreeD receiver in parallel — phase-locked to the ARKit frame, not a timer.

NDI in & out

Send your AR feed over NDI to your switcher, and bring NDI sources back as an in-app overlay — all over your local network.

VRM avatars in AR

Import any .vrm and render it in your space with a stylized cel look, live lighting, and ground contact shadows.

Face & motion tracking

ARKit blendshapes drive expressions, with full perfect-sync support — or let Warudo's VMC feed take over the face and body.

Record takes

Capture the composited AR view to HEVC video with audio and save straight to your Photos library. Orientation locks for the whole take.

See it on device

Built natively in SwiftUI with a Liquid-Glass interface modeled on a real cinema camera.
