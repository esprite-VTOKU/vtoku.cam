What it does

An AR camera and VTuber rig on its own, with real bonded streaming when you go live.

AR camera tracking

ARKit world tracking follows your device in real space, so moving the phone moves the shot. The field of view comes from the real camera lens.

Perform as a VTuber

Bring any VRM model and become your VTuber in AR, with a hand-tuned cel and toon look. Sample avatars are built in, so it works the moment you open the app.

Real-time face tracking

The front camera drives your expressions live with ARKit face tracking, including full perfect-sync blendshapes when your model supports them.

Idle and dance motion

Play built-in idle and dance animations, or import your own VRM animations as .vrma files.

Record takes to Photos

Record video with audio straight to your Photos library. The interface orientation stays locked for the whole take, so a mid-take rotation can't spoil the shot.

Bonded live streaming

Go live over SRT, and bond several connections at once with SRTLA so your stream holds up on real-world networks. Combine Wi-Fi and cellular for true on-the-go VRL.

Open it and shoot

You don't need any extra software or hardware to start. Grant the camera, load a bundled avatar, and the AR viewfinder is live. Record a take and it lands in Photos.

Plug into your studio

Stream camera pose over VMC/OSC and FreeD into Warudo, drive your avatar back from Warudo over VMC, exchange NDI video on your network, and receive iFacialMocap face data. All optional, all off by default. See the docs.

On device

Built for iPhone and iPad, with a camera-style interface that feels right from the first shot.

Your footage stays yours

Camera, microphone, and face data are processed on device. Recordings save to your own Photos library, and the streaming features send data only to receivers and servers you choose. VTOKU collects nothing. Read the privacy policy.
